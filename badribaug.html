Structural Repairs and Rehabilitation Works

• Client Requirements:

  • Rehabilitation of structure to retain it for 15-20 years, New RCC and MS Components to be added and Civil Finishes.
  • External Crack filling, waterproof texture and Paint.

• Existing Conditions:

  • Concrete was spelled off the structural members - columns were mainly damaged through out the length - beams were sagging.
  • Slab concrete was also loose and at some places was spelled off.
  • Steel stirrups in all Columns and beams were rusted and only thin portions were left 2.
  • Parapet on 5th floor utility side was completely damaged.
  • Water seepage was also observed on floor below terrace and through walls at all floors.
LOCATION Mumbai

STRUCTURAL CONSULTANT Al Mohandis
ENGINEERING TEAM Bunyan Constructions

Project Details

Remedial Measures:

As Structure was observed to be RCC Frame structure and in order to strengthen a frame structure it's main components i.e. Columns, Beams and Slab should be repaired The process to repair columns, beam or slab is as follows:

  • Remove plaster so as to find all cracks and loose concrete
  • Spelled concrete should be removed along with concrete behind reinforcement
  • Treatment of Reinforcement is most important part of repairs, if not done properly and moisture reaches the steel carbonation takes place and will again damage structure's life span
  • Treatment of reinforcement includes cleaning of rust layers by wire brush or any mechanical means followed by passivators to remove rust, usually passivators needs to be applied in intervals of 6hrs for perfect results and cleaning again with wire brush.
  • After complete rust removal a layer of cementitious rust preventer is to be applied. (Dosage as per manufacturers instructions)
  • Apply rust preventer on new bars if any.
  • In case of new bars drill hole of length = 10D, 10 times Dia. of bar, clear holes with all dust and dirt particles with mechanical air blower, fill hole completely with Chemical Grout and insert the bar.
  • Above process is same in every method as it's a key process to attain desired results
Further treatment can be calssified in two types i.e
  1. Jacketing
    • Concrete Jacketing (for cover greater than 100mm thick)
    • Microconcrete Jacketing (for cover less than 100mm thick)
  2. Polymer Modified Mortar (for cover less than 40mm thick)
Polymer Modified Mortar (PMM) :

Proportion used to make PMM should be 1:5:15 (Chemical : Cement : Quartz Sand) Note:

  1. Add extra cement for self bonding.
  2. Bonding agent to be applied of approved make (MYK, Araldite or equivalent)
Execution:

"Expect the best, plan for the worst, and prepare to be surprised." ~ Denis Waitley


Our first job on any site is to plan our activities, which helps us to achieve 3 most important things:

  1. Quality
  2. Time
  3. Economy

As it was an active site we had constrains to work on single floor at a time.

And the challenging part was to protect pedestrian from any damage.

A full double scaffold was erected with green net cover and base platform was built to stop debris fallout.


We started with top floor, first activity was to remove all claddings and plaster to expose concrete surface, all columns and beams were completely damaged and exposed to rust so we had provide additional support below beams.
There were many extra works along with structural repair works,

  • We had to construct new RCC sun shades with support of additional support beam.
  • Our consultant suggested to support utility side of building with MS sections - Chemical Grout of Hikoki (Hitachi) Mongu was used.
  • A Cantilever over hang storage cabinet of RCC was constructed.

Moving forward to lower floors along with repairs, MS works, RCC works, we started AAC Block masonry works of 100mm and 200mm thick, AAC Blocks used were Magicrete, and ready plaster of ACC.

Conclusion:

Retrofitting and Miscellaneous works were successfully executed in the time span of 9 months, using


  1. Sika Microconcrete: ~45,000 Kgs
  2. Quartz sand: ~700 kgs
  3. Steel Fe-500: ~2,500 kgs
  4. Concrete M25: ~10 Cum
  5. MS Sections: ~4 MT
  6. AAC Blocks: ~8000 Blocks
  7. ACC Ready Plast: ~1500 Bags
  8. External Paint: ~30,000 Sft
  9. Project value: 1.04 Crore
