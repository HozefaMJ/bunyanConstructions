Structural Rehabilitation of Office Building of Simplex Group of Companies

• Job Description:

The 3 Storeyed RCC Structured Building is located at Byculla East Besides Godrej Sky, South Mumbai. The approximate Age of the structure as informed by on-site personnel was known to be around 40 years.


• Client Requirements:

There was a joint Site survey meeting held wherein the present condition of the site were jointly observed by Bunyan Constructions and Project Engineer Mr. P. Murugesan. The Following were some of the observations Noted:-

  • External Plaster at outer walls of Staircase Headroom Comprising of LMR had deteriorated with a Large number of Cracks resulting in Water seepage on the inside.
  • The Concrete of Circular columns had spalled at all levels in different columns which needed Immediate treatment.
  • Concrete from Beams had also been Spalled off at Terrace level and Ground Floor level requiring proper treatment.
  • It was further observed that the exposed steel was Highly Rusted and that the Stirrups were eaten up due to rusting.
  • Water seepage was also observed on floor below terrace and through walls at all floors.
LOCATION Mumbai
CLIENT Anjuman-E-Shiate-Ali
STRUCTURAL CONSULTANT Al Mohandis
ENGINEERING TEAM Bunyan Constructions
SCOPE Rehabilitation & Retrofitting Works
COMPLETED February 2019
SIZE 30,000 sq feet

Project Details

Remedial Measures:
  1. The areas where the plaster had sounded to be hollow and cracks were visible, we suggested chipping of old plaster and redoing with a fresh new Plaster in CM 1:4
  2. Structural repairs were majorly executed by either of the two following methods :-
    • Micro concrete jacketing for members where the depth of filling was more than 100mm
    • Polymer modified mortar for members where the depth of rupture was less than 40mm.
Execution:

We first began our job of works by starting with the external plaster to the Staircase headroom at the terrace. This was urgent as external cracks and loosened plaster led to massive Water seepage on the staircase and in the lift lobby as well. We began with chipping off old plaster carefully and stacking the debris in gunny bags which were later lowered down to the Ground floor. The main challenge was to complete the plastering with an extensive amount of rain resulting in washing off of the day’s work. Precautions were taken and the same was completed satisfactorily.


On exposure of bare walls after chipping off the plaster at two places it was observed that the RCC beam had deteriorated asking for Repairs on to it. We then introduced new steel Stirrups to the beam which were grouted with MYK grout for further strengthening the beam section. Similarly, all the exposed beams were strengthened and were then recast by Polymer Modified Mortar.

We completed the works at the terrace as it was a matter of urgency and then moved down to the ground floor for the Rehabilitation of the Structural Members.

On the ground floor, we had observed maximum columns that had spalling of concrete. The amount of spalling only suggested that we would have to Recast them with Micro-concrete. The challenge out here was that the structure comprised of Round Columns throughout from top to bottom. We were very clear that we will be casting them again in the Round shape so as to maintain the uniformity of the structure.

So we started the repair works of Ground floor columns and we followed the following steps in the repair process:-

  1. Breaking and removing old loose spalled-off concrete and loose concrete.
  2. Removing Rust from old bars and Applying a coat of Rust passivator i.e. Sunanda Rusticide.
  3. Re cleaning of Rusted bars and applying a second coat of Sunanda Rusticide. And thereafter washing the member so as to remove any acidic residual therein.
  4. Introducing New steel Stirrups or Main bars as and where required.
  5. Applying a cementitious coat of Rust Preventor i.e Sunanda Fixoprime on Old as well as New Steel bars.
  6. Applying a bond coat of Sunanda EP before placing shutters.
  7. Shuttering of round columns using GI metal sheet of 22 gauge.
  8. Placement of Sika Micro Concrete by site mix procedure.
  9. In the case of PMM, a mortar inappropriate mix proportion was made along with Sunanda EP, and the same was placed by plastering.

Thus the entire project was completed successfully with Zero Hazards and with proper Environment safety within a span of 2 months.

Conclusion:

Retrofitting and Miscellaneous works were successfully executed in the time span of 9 months, using


  1. Sika Microconcrete: ~45,000 Kgs
  2. Quartz sand: ~700 kgs
  3. Steel Fe-500: ~2,500 kgs
  4. Concrete M25: ~10 Cum
  5. MS Sections: ~4 MT
  6. AAC Blocks: ~8000 Blocks
  7. ACC Ready Plast: ~1500 Bags
  8. External Paint: ~30,000 Sft
  9. Project value: 1.04 Crore
